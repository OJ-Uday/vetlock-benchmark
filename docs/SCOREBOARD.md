## Honesty notes

- **All scanners are run against ALL fixtures.** A scanner that's not installed in the run environment records `errored`, not `skipped`. That's the truth.
- **`caught`** = the scanner returned non-CLEAN AND at least one finding is on the malicious package.
- **`partial`** = non-CLEAN but findings are on unrelated packages (adjacency false-positive that happens to hit the affected version).
- **`missed`** = CLEAN or non-CLEAN on wrong packages entirely.
- **`errored`** = unhandled crash. A graceful "unsupported format" is `missed`, not `errored`.
- **vetlock is scored by the same rules as every other scanner.** vetlock's misses appear here too.

If your scanner is missing or scored badly and you think that's wrong, [open an issue](https://github.com/OJ-Uday/vetlock-benchmark/issues) or send a PR against `scorers/<your-scanner>.js`.
